Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert made of layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, creamy mascarpone filling, and a dusting of cocoa powder. Its name means "pick-me-up," a nod to its espresso base.

This version is simple to prepare yet indulgent, perfect for impressing guests or treating yourself after a special meal.

Ingredients

  • 250g mascarpone cheese
  • 200g ladyfingers (savoiardi biscuits)
  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 100g granulated sugar
  • 200ml strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 2 tbsp coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 50ml heavy cream (optional for extra richness)
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional garnish)

Steps

  1. Brew strong coffee and let it cool. Mix with coffee liqueur if using.
  2. Whisk egg yolks with sugar until pale and creamy. Fold in mascarpone and whipped cream (if using).
  3. In a separate bowl, beat egg whites until stiff peaks form. Gently fold them into the mascarpone mixture.
  4. Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the cooled coffee and arrange in a layer in a dish.
  5. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers. Repeat with another layer of soaked biscuits and cream.
  6. Dust the top with cocoa powder and chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours (overnight is best).
  7. Before serving, garnish with extra cocoa powder or chocolate shavings.
